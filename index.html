SOLEY

Physics-Based PV Yield Engine

From Nanometers to Gigawatts

The first photovoltaic software that bridges atomic-scale device physics and utility-scale energy yield. Simulate the annual production of theoretical devices (Perovskites, Tandems) with the same speed as commercial silicon tools.

The Gap

Device Simulators (e.g., SCAPS) are physically accurate but too slow for annual simulations.

Yield Simulators (e.g., PVsyst) are fast but empirical—they require a datasheet of an existing panel. You cannot simulate what you haven't built.

The SOLEY Bridge

SOLEY uses Bottom-Up Logic. It calculates Optical Absorption (A) and solves Detailed Balance for every timestep.

Result: Change a layer thickness by 10nm and immediately see the impact on LCOE and Annual Yield (kWh).

For Education

  • Beyond "Efficiency": Teaches students that efficiency is dynamic, not a static datasheet number.
  • Full Transparency: Trace the logic from Maxwell's Equations (TMM) to Grid Injection.
  • Geography Matters: Same roughness yields different results in London (diffuse) vs Cairo (direct).

For Industry

  • Tandem Optimization: Recalculates current matching for every hour based on the real solar spectrum (Red/Blue ratio).
  • "Fail Fast": Simulate 1,000 coating variations in software before fabricating a single physical prototype.
  • Production Ready: Includes real-world losses: Shading, Soiling, Aging, and Tracking (1-axis/2-axis).
